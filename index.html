About Me

Hello! My name is Arthur Silva Magalhães, and I am currently a MSc student in Astronomy at Universidade de São Paulo (USP), where I also earned my BSc in Physics in 2023. I work under the supervision of Prof. Jane Gregorio-Hetem, and my research interests primarily focus on star clusters, stellar formation, and the intersection of computational and observational astronomy using photometry and spectroscopy. During my master's program, I worked on characterizing the open clusters Collinder 205 and Ruprecht 79 using infrared SOAR data collected with the Spartan IR camera.

During my exchange semester at Uppsala University (spring term of 2025), I worked under the supervision of Prof. Andreas Korn, where I analyzed VLT FLAMES-UVES data to investigate new atomic diffusion trends in the globular cluster NGC 6397. I also gained experience in differential analysis by studying the solar twin M67-1194 in the open cluster M67.

I am a proud Brazilian, and although I live in São Paulo, I was born and grew up in Belo Horizonte, the capital of Minas Gerais state. Apart from academia, I am passionate about a number of subjects and have many hobbies: sports (Formula 1, football, and rugby), music (acoustic guitar, rap, and samba), and reading (mainly magical realism, philosophical fiction, and sci-fi). I also really enjoy trying new beers throughout the world and sharing them with good company!

Research

My research mainly focus on star clusters (the best objects in the universe), and the intersection between observational and computational astrophysics. During my undergraduate and master's research I worked with infrared data of young open star clusters and the analysis of its Pre-Main Sequence stars. While, during my exchange semester at Uppsala University, I worked with globular clusters and the abundance analysis of metal poor-stars.

Below you can find a summary of my research and my main interests:

Open Star Clusters

Infrared Photometry

During my Master’s research, I studied the young open clusters Collinder 205 and Ruprecht 79 using near-infrared observations from the Spartan camera on the SOAR Telescope (Chile!), complemented by public data from the 2MASS catalog. The Spartan images revealed faint stars not detected by 2MASS and also better resolved stars close together. Therefore, through a linear transformation between the photometric systems, I was able to refine and expanded the 2MASS dataset.

Outreach

Independent YouTube Content Creator

I run an independent YouTube channel where I mainly talk about life in academia, astrophysics, study tips, and software tutorials. The videos are recorded in Portuguese, and the objective is to inspire young Brazilian students to pursue scientific careers by sharing my real-life experiences, achievements, and struggles.

Check out my latest video!

Astropontos

Astropontos is the Portuguese equivalent of Astrobites. It is an astronomy blog where graduate students can post summaries of the latest papers on arXiv Astro-ph. The goal is to make recent astronomy developments easily accessible to undergraduate students and aspiring researchers. The website also includes posts on different topics as conference results, grad school tips, and tutorials. I've been an active member since 2025.
